# EU and Netherlands Privacy and Legal Research

Use this skill for legal-readiness research concerning the VANTAM website and operations.

This skill supports research and implementation preparation. It does not provide legal approval and must not present generated conclusions as professional Dutch legal advice.

## Primary-source requirement

Prioritise:

1. EUR-Lex
2. Official EU legislation
3. European Data Protection Board
4. Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens
5. ACM
6. ConsuWijzer
7. Business.gov.nl
8. Rijksoverheid
9. Belastingdienst
10. Official Dutch legislation and government guidance

Do not rely on:

- generic policy generators;
- law-firm marketing summaries when official sources exist;
- blog posts;
- template websites;
- forum answers;
- uncited AI summaries.

## Source record

For every material legal conclusion record:

- source title;
- issuing organisation;
- source URL or official reference;
- publication, update, or effective date;
- access date;
- exact verified rule or guidance;
- applicability to VANTAM;
- uncertainty;
- owner action;
- whether professional review is required.

## Required distinctions

Always distinguish:

- legal requirement;
- regulatory guidance;
- industry practice;
- owner decision;
- drafting recommendation;
- unresolved question;
- professional legal-review item.

Do not describe a recommendation as mandatory law.

## Privacy audit areas

Assess:

- controller identity;
- contact details;
- purposes;
- lawful bases;
- data categories;
- special-category data risk;
- recipients;
- processors;
- independent controllers;
- international transfers;
- retention;
- deletion;
- backups;
- security wording;
- data-subject rights;
- privacy requests;
- identity verification;
- complaints;
- children and minors where relevant;
- automated decisions;
- cookies and tracking;
- policy versioning;
- incident and breach handling;
- processor agreements;
- records of processing where relevant.

## Terms readiness areas

Assess:

- identity of the contracting party;
- geographical address requirements;
- VAT-status implications;
- B2C and B2B distinction;
- pre-contract information;
- contract formation;
- durable-medium confirmation;
- prices;
- payment;
- cancellation;
- refunds;
- rescheduling;
- withdrawal rights;
- early commencement;
- proportional payment;
- complaints;
- liability;
- third-party decisions;
- force majeure;
- document hierarchy;
- governing law;
- dispute handling;
- service-language hierarchy;
- housing-support and rental-mediation risk.

## Public-copy rule

Public legal copy must be understandable to a normal visitor.

Do not expose:

- developer notes;
- repository uncertainty;
- account speculation;
- security-sensitive implementation details;
- internal legal debates;
- private owner information not authorised for publication.

## Final restriction

Do not draft final Privacy Policy or Terms during an audit-readiness task unless the user explicitly starts that drafting phase.

Do not claim compliance, enforceability, or legal approval.
