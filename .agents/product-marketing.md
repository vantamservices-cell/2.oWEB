# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-18*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** VANTAM is student-led practical support for international students in the Netherlands.

**What it does:** VANTAM helps international students work through the practical steps around relocation, housing, arrival preparation and follow-up settlement. The site shows a student-led public ladder, a separate housing activation path, and a contact flow that pre-fills the right context before the visitor reaches out.

**Product category:** Practical relocation support and coordination service.

**Product type:** Service business.

**Business model:** Paid consultations, public packages and housing activation services.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Primarily students and other private individuals; also universities, education consultants, HR teams and agencies that may refer students.

**Decision-makers:** International students, parents or family supporters, and organisational referrers.

**Primary use case:** Getting the right practical support for the next step without having to piece together the Dutch process alone.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Understand what to do next and in what order.
- Choose between consultation, main ladder or housing activation.
- Reduce uncertainty around housing, arrival planning and follow-up setup.

**Use cases:**
- Relocation orientation before arrival.
- Main-ladder support for arrival and first-year setup.
- Housing-ready or housing-search campaigns as a separate activation path.
- One-off help with documents, communication and practical coordination.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Students moving to the Netherlands often need help turning a messy relocation process into a clear sequence of practical steps.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Official sources are fragmented and hard to navigate.
- Generic relocation advice is too broad.
- Licensed professionals are not the right fit for practical coordination tasks.

**What it costs them:** Time, stress, missed steps, avoidable fees and uncertainty for the student and the people supporting them.

**Emotional tension:** Confusion, fear of making a mistake, pressure to act quickly and concern that something important will be missed.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Other relocation support services - may be broader or less transparent about scope and limits.

**Secondary:** Doing it alone - free, but time-consuming and easy to get wrong.

**Indirect:** Asking friends, family or forum threads - useful sometimes, but inconsistent and not tailored to the exact situation.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Student-led positioning instead of a generic relocation story.
- Clear split between consultation, main ladder and housing activation.
- Transparent boundaries: VANTAM does not present itself as legal, financial, insurance or real-estate advice.
- Multilingual visitor experience in Ukrainian, Russian and English.

**How we do it differently:** We explain the process in plain language, prefill the contact form with the selected context and make the next step obvious before people contact us.

**Why that's better:** It reduces confusion and helps people choose the right level of support faster.

**Why customers choose us:** They want a calm, local and practical point of contact instead of piecing the process together themselves.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I only need help with one step." | Use a single service or housing activation instead of a larger package. |
| "I do not know if I need the main ladder." | Start with Relocation Orientation and get a clearer recommendation. |
| "Can you guarantee housing or an outcome?" | No. VANTAM supports preparation, explanation and coordination, but cannot guarantee third-party outcomes. |

**Anti-persona:** People looking for legal representation, financial advice, insurance brokering, real-estate agency services or guaranteed outcomes.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Confusing Dutch systems, time pressure and fear of missing a step.

**Pull:** A local contact who can explain the process and help with the next action.

**Habit:** Doing everything themselves, asking friends or waiting until a problem becomes urgent.

**Anxiety:** Worry about cost, scope, what happens after contact and whether the support is relevant.
