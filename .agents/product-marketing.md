# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-18*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** VANTAM is practical support for international students from arrival planning to everyday student life in the Netherlands.

**What it does:** VANTAM helps international students understand the right sequence, choose the right support level, and work through defined relocation, housing, first-year, and practical-problem situations. The public system is built around one consultation entry, two main packages, a separate housing path, and defined individual student services.

**Product category:** Practical student relocation and coordination support.

**Product type:** Service business.

**Business model:** Paid consultation, fixed-price packages, fixed-price and request-priced student services, and housing support offers with explicit execution limits.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Primarily individual international students; secondarily parents/families and organisations that refer students.

**Decision-makers:** International students, sometimes supported by parents/family, and organisational referrers.

**Primary use case:** Getting a clear next step and the right support scope for relocation, housing, arrival setup, first-year administration, or a defined practical problem in the Netherlands.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Understand what happens next and in what order.
- Choose the right product without overbuying.
- Turn a practical student problem into a defined action sequence with visible outputs.

**Use cases:**
- Relocation Orientation before committing to a package.
- VANTAM Start for first-month setup.
- VANTAM First Year for defined support through the first academic year.
- Housing Ready or Housing Search Campaign for housing preparation and campaign execution.
- Individual Student Services for practical problems already in progress.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Students moving to or already living in the Netherlands often do not know which practical step comes next, what is urgent, or where scope ends.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Official information is fragmented and hard to sequence.
- Friends/forums are inconsistent and not case-specific.
- Regulated specialists are often not the right first layer for practical coordination.

**What it costs them:** Stress, missed deadlines, extra housing/admin costs, family uncertainty, and slow recovery from practical problems.

**Emotional tension:** Confusion, urgency, fear of making a mistake, and concern that something important will be missed.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Practical relocation support providers - often broader, less student-specific, or less explicit about limits.

**Secondary:** Doing it alone with official sites and ad hoc help - free, but fragmented and risky.

**Indirect:** Friends, family, forum threads, or partner referrals without follow-through - easy to access but inconsistent.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Student-specific positioning instead of generic relocation language.
- Clear public ladder: one consultation, one flagship package, separate housing path, defined individual services.
- Visible deliverables and fixed execution limits.
- Transparent boundaries around regulated topics and third-party outcomes.
- Multilingual public experience in English, Ukrainian, and Russian.

**How we do it differently:** We present a clear product system, route visitors by situation, prefill the contact form with the exact service context, and explain scope through structured outcomes and deliverables rather than vague support language.

**Why that's better:** It reduces decision friction, prevents overpromising, and helps visitors see what they actually receive.

**Why customers choose us:** They want a calm, local, practical contact who can organise the next step without pretending to be a portal, agency, or regulated advisor.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I only need help with one problem." | Use Individual Student Services or the housing path instead of a larger package. |
| "I do not know whether I need Start or First Year." | Start with Relocation Orientation and get a product recommendation plus a written recap. |
| "Can you guarantee housing or an approval?" | No. VANTAM helps with preparation, sequence, coordination, and communication, but not third-party decisions. |

**Anti-persona:** People looking for guaranteed outcomes, legal representation, immigration advice, insurance brokering, financial advice, real-estate agency services, or a client portal.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Fragmented systems, time pressure, housing risk, official letters, and first-year admin complexity.

**Pull:** One practical contact, clear sequence, visible deliverables, and defined service limits.

**Habit:** Waiting too long, trying to solve everything alone, or asking multiple informal sources.

**Anxiety:** Cost, scope, whether the help is relevant, and whether support will stay practical instead of vague.

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**
- "I do not know what to do first."
- "I already live here and have a practical problem."
- "Can someone check this housing offer?"
- "I need help with a letter, registration, or university administration."

**How they describe us:**
- "A practical local contact."
- "Someone who can explain the steps."
- "Support with the next action."

**Words to use:**
practical, clear, next step, action plan, support, housing, registration, first year, student, coordination, review, recap, roadmap

**Words to avoid:**
guaranteed, seamless, compliant-sounding filler, best, leading, legal advice, financial advice, insurance advice, real-estate agency, portal, dashboard, promise
