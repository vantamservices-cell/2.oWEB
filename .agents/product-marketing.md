# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-13*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** VANTAM is practical housing and relocation support for international people before and after arrival in the Netherlands.

**What it does:** VANTAM helps people understand and complete practical setup steps before or after arrival: housing preparation and application support, registration, BSN, DigiD, banking, insurance, healthcare, university admin, official letters and housing-related communication or document review. The site also helps visitors compare consultations, single services and packages before they contact VANTAM.

**Product category:** Practical relocation support / setup coordination service.

**Product type:** Service business.

**Business model:** Paid consultations, single services and fixed-price packages.

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Primarily individuals; also agencies, HR teams, universities and education consultants that may refer clients.

**Decision-makers:** International students, expats and professionals, families supporting the move, and organisational partners.

**Primary use case:** Getting through the first practical steps in the Netherlands without missing something important.

**Jobs to be done:**
- Understand what needs to happen, in what order.
- Prepare the right documents and next actions.
- Reduce uncertainty around administration, housing questions and everyday setup.

**Use cases:**
- Before arrival planning.
- First weeks after arrival.
- Already in the Netherlands and still sorting practical setup.
- One-off help with BSN, DigiD, bank, insurance, GP, letters, university admin, housing application support, rental-contract support or housing communication.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** People arrive in the Netherlands without enough local knowledge to confidently handle the required setup steps.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Official sources are often unclear, fragmented or hard to navigate.
- Generic relocation advice is too broad.
- Licensed professionals are not the right fit for simple practical setup questions.

**What it costs them:** Time, stress, missed steps, avoidable fees and uncertainty for the person moving and the people supporting them.

**Emotional tension:** Confusion, fear of making a mistake, pressure to act quickly and worry about whether the move is under control.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Other relocation support services - may be broader or less transparent about scope and limits.

**Secondary:** Doing it alone - free, but time-consuming and easy to get wrong.

**Indirect:** Asking friends, family or forum threads - helpful sometimes, but inconsistent and not tailored to the exact situation.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Clear separation between consultation, single services and packages.
- Practical help focused on the first months in the Netherlands.
- Transparent boundaries: VANTAM does not pose as legal, financial, insurance or real-estate advice.
- Multilingual visitor experience in Ukrainian, Russian and English.

**How we do it differently:** We explain the process in plain language, prefill the contact form with the chosen context and make the next step obvious before people contact us.

**Why that's better:** It reduces confusion and helps people choose the right level of support faster.

**Why customers choose us:** They want a calm, local and practical point of contact instead of piecing the process together themselves.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I only need help with one step." | Use a single service instead of a package. |
| "I don't know if I need a package." | Start with a consultation and get a clearer recommendation. |
| "Can you guarantee housing or an official result?" | No. VANTAM supports preparation, explanation and communication, but cannot guarantee third-party outcomes. |

**Anti-persona:** People looking for legal representation, financial advice, insurance brokering, real-estate agency services or guaranteed housing outcomes.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Confusing Dutch systems, time pressure and fear of missing a step.

**Pull:** A local contact who can explain the process and help with the next action.

**Habit:** Doing everything themselves, asking friends, or waiting until a problem gets urgent.

**Anxiety:** Worry about cost, scope, what happens after contact and whether the support is actually relevant.

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**
- "I don't know where to start."
- "I need help with BSN / DigiD / registration."
- "Can someone check this housing offer?"
- "I want to know what my child needs to do first."

**How they describe us:**
- "A local coordinator."
- "Practical help."
- "Someone who can explain the steps."

**Words to use:**
practical, local, clear, next step, registration, support, consultation, single service, package, housing context, university admin

**Words to avoid:**
official, guaranteed, best, leading, seamless, compliant-sounding filler, legal advice, financial advice, insurance advice, real estate agency, promise

**Glossary:**
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| BSN | Dutch citizen service number required for many setup steps |
| DigiD | Dutch digital identity used for public services |
| GP / huisarts | General practitioner / family doctor |

| Prepare | Package for pre-arrival planning |
| Thrive | Package for more complex, higher-touch support |
