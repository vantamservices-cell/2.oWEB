# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

VANTAM serves people who need practical help navigating relocation and early life in the Netherlands:

- International students who are preparing to arrive, completing registration, or settling into study and daily life.
- Young expats who need a clear route through Dutch administration, healthcare, banking, insurance, housing communication, and official correspondence.
- Parents and families who want visibility, reduced uncertainty, and a trusted local point of coordination for a student or young adult.
- Education and relocation partners who may refer clients needing hands-on, multilingual support.

Visitors usually arrive with limited local knowledge, time pressure, unfamiliar terminology, and a high cost of making avoidable administrative mistakes. Their core job is to understand what must happen, in what order, what VANTAM can handle, and what remains their responsibility.

## Product Purpose

VANTAM is a Netherlands-based support and coordination service for housing context, relocation, expat administration, and student adaptation. The website explains the offer, establishes trust, helps visitors compare consultations, individual services, and packages, and converts an informed choice into a contact request.

The service is practical rather than advisory in a regulated sense. VANTAM prepares documents, explains processes and correspondence, coordinates steps, and supports communication. It does not present itself as a law firm, financial adviser, insurance broker, or housing agency. Housing support is contextual guidance, rental-document explanation, scam-risk awareness, and communication support, not a promise to find accommodation.

Success means that a visitor can quickly identify the right level of help, understand the price and limits before contacting VANTAM, use the calculator or adaptation planner without confusion, and submit a request with realistic expectations.

## Brand Personality

**Calm, capable, transparent.**

VANTAM should feel like a trusted local coordinator who has already organized the next steps. The voice is direct, reassuring, practical, and precise. It explains unfamiliar systems in plain language without sounding casual, bureaucratic, alarmist, or legally authoritative.

The emotional outcome is informed confidence: students should feel less overwhelmed, parents should feel greater visibility and control, and expats should feel that the process has become manageable. Premium means attentiveness, clarity, and dependable structure, not luxury theatre.

All three language versions, Ukrainian, Russian, and English, must communicate the same offer, prices, limitations, and level of respect.

## Anti-references

- Monochrome, flat administrative interfaces.
- A continuous wall of identical outlined cards.
- Tiny uppercase or condensed copy used for important content.
- Crowded navigation with every destination in one row.
- Generic SaaS or AI-marketing visuals.
- Dark dashboard styling across the entire site.
- Stock-photo relocation clichés, fake trust signals, invented guarantees, statistics, reviews, licences, partners, or awards.
- Aggressive sales language, fear-based pressure, or claims that blur VANTAM's professional boundaries.

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence through clarity.** Make scope, sequence, price, format, inclusions, exclusions, and next actions visible before decorative detail.
2. **Practical before promotional.** Demonstrate value through useful explanations, tools, checklists, and transparent boundaries rather than inflated claims.
3. **One brand, two reassurance needs.** Give students and young expats autonomy while giving parents and families enough structure to trust the process.
4. **Human guidance, structured delivery.** The experience should combine the warmth of a local contact with the order and precision of a well-run service.
5. **Multilingual parity.** Ukrainian, Russian, and English are equal product experiences; no language may receive reduced content, weaker hierarchy, or broken interaction states.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, keyboard operation, focus visibility, form labeling, semantic structure, and touch targets. Body text must remain readable on standard laptop screens without zooming, and important meaning must never depend on color alone.

Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`, preserve clear active and selected states, and keep interactive tools usable by keyboard. Typography and layouts must support both Latin and Cyrillic scripts without clipping, artificial condensation, or excessive uppercase. Responsive behavior must be intentionally designed for desktop, laptop, tablet, and mobile rather than achieved by shrinking the desktop layout.
