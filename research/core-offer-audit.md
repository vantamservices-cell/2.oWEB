# VANTAM Core Offer Audit

Date: 2026-06-13

## Scope

Reviewed the current website offer, public competitor pages and public guidance sources for the Netherlands housing / relocation / newcomer support market. The goal was not to rank the market exhaustively, but to validate whether VANTAM has a coherent, commercially understandable offer for international newcomers and residents navigating housing and practical settlement in the Netherlands.

Correction note: the sample used for this audit was housing-heavy, so the housing findings should be read as one part of the offer context rather than evidence that VANTAM needs to become a housing-only brand.

- Isolated paid consultations or document checks

## Key Customer Patterns

- People want to know the next step, not only a list of services.
- Housing anxiety is usually tied to competition, guarantors, documents and fear of missing a detail.
- Before-arrival planning and after-arrival catch-up are both common needs.
- Customers want plain-language guidance and a single local contact.
- Many buyers are willing to pay for help, but they want clear boundaries and no fake guarantee.
- People often self-segment by situation: arriving soon, already in the Netherlands, housing found, housing not found, family support, or organisational referral.

## Verified VANTAM Strengths

- Clear three-part offer structure: consultation, single service and package.
- Strong housing and relocation relevance before and after arrival.
- Explicit boundaries: no legal, financial, insurance or real-estate role.
- Multilingual site experience in Ukrainian, Russian and English.
- Situation selector and form prefill reduce friction.
- Housing support is visible and understandable instead of hidden inside generic relocation wording.

## Current Offer Breakdown

### 1. Confirmed services and capabilities

- Consultations for relocation orientation, family confidence and urgent situations.
- Single services for registration / BSN, DigiD, banking, insurance, letters, university admin, rental-contract explanation, housing scam check, deposit return, insurance claim, healthcare registration and landlord communication.
- A housing preparation and application support service that is remote-only and assessment-based.
- Packages: Prepare, Arrival Setup and Thrive.
- Partner enquiry path for agencies, HR teams, universities and education consultants.
- Calculator, checklist / planner, FAQ and contact qualification flow.

### 2. Confirmed prices and package inclusions

- Consultations are publicly priced at `€59`, `€79` and `€99`.
- Most single services show fixed prices.
- Housing Preparation & Application Support is marked `After assessment`.
- Prepare is `€349`.
- Arrival Setup is `€749`.
- Thrive is `€1690`.
- Package inclusions are publicly listed on the site, including time windows and support items for each package.

### 3. Unclear or incomplete offers

- Housing Preparation & Application Support remains quote-based after assessment, but the exact pricing model is not yet stated as policy.
- The boundary between practical contract explanation and legal-style contract review is not fully explicit.
- The public rules for payment timing, cancellation and refunds are not finalised.
- Delivery and response-time promises are implied but not clearly published as policy.
- Thrive contains several high-commitment inclusions that should be confirmed before treating them as final facts.

### 4. Repeated or overlapping offers

- Consultations overlap with single services when the customer only needs help choosing the next step.
- Housing Preparation & Application Support overlaps with rental-contract explanation, housing scam check and landlord communication.
- Arrival Setup overlaps with several standalone setup services, especially registration, DigiD, banking, insurance and healthcare.
- Thrive overlaps with Arrival Setup plus several advanced housing and support add-ons.

### 5. Unsupported claims

- No obvious false claims were found in the high-level positioning.
- The main risk is not headline dishonesty, but treating unconfirmed package inclusions and service promises as settled facts.

### 6. Decisions requiring owner confirmation

- Whether Housing Preparation & Application Support is fixed-price or quote-based.
- Whether rental-contract support is explanation only or a deeper review scope.
- Whether agency communication is drafting support only or direct follow-up on the client’s behalf.
- Whether consultations can be credited toward packages within 14 days.
- Whether Arrival Setup has a formal payment, cancellation and refund policy that should be public.
- Whether Arrival Setup and Thrive should publish response-time or delivery-time commitments.
- Whether Thrive really includes physical accompaniment, weekly parent updates, urgent response slots, deposit-return help, insurance-claim help and room-setup coordination.
- Whether a public partnership response-time promise should exist.

## Highest-Impact Gaps

- The homepage is clearer than before, but the offer hierarchy still needs a fully confirmed boundary between consultation, single service, package and custom assessment work.
- The site needs one more level of clarity around what happens after contact and which promises are written policy versus indicative timing.
- Package differentiation is good, but Thrive currently carries the most confirmation risk.
- The partnership path is useful, but it should remain framed as referral / handoff support unless a formal partner programme is approved.

## Implemented Improvements

- Updated the public positioning copy to reflect the three service pillars across the journey.
- Tightened the audience wording in Ukrainian, Russian and English.
- Strengthened the consultation framing as the easiest way to identify the right next step.
- Replaced the vague general-enquiry label with a more natural `Not sure yet / general question` variant.
- Clarified the FAQ answer so consultations, single services and packages are distinct.
- Updated the metadata description to match the revised public positioning.
- Updated the internal product-marketing context to match the verified offer language.
- Expanded the owner-confirmation file so unresolved package and delivery decisions are explicit.

## Post-Launch Opportunities

- Add a concise public policy note for payment, cancellation and refunds once the owner confirms it.
- Add more proof or scenario examples if the team can verify them.
- Review whether the contact form can be shortened after launch data shows where people hesitate.
