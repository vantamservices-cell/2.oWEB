# Manual email sequence

- Enquiry acknowledgement: purpose, confirm receipt and next step; attachments, none or the requested info sheet; required evidence, sent email and timestamp; CRM change, `new` to `contacted` or `spam`; wording to avoid, contract language, promises, or pricing commitments.
- Consultation confirmation: purpose, confirm time, fee, reschedule rule, and payment timing; attachments, consultation confirmation; required evidence, versioned PDF and sent copy; CRM change, `consultation_proposed` to `consultation_booked`; wording to avoid, no-show fee, urgency pressure, or guaranteed outcome.
- Consultation invoice: purpose, request payment before the consultation; attachments, invoice and payment details; required evidence, invoice number and sent email; CRM change, keep `consultation_booked`; wording to avoid, immediate-service language or open-ended billing.
- Offer delivery: purpose, send the written service offer; attachments, service offer appendix; required evidence, exact version sent; CRM change, `consultation_booked` to `offer_sent`; wording to avoid, approval-by-silence or outcome guarantees.
- Terms and withdrawal-information delivery: purpose, attach the applicable Terms, withdrawal information, model form, and early-start request when relevant; attachments, versioned legal packet; required evidence, attachment list and timestamp; CRM change, keep `offer_sent`; wording to avoid, mixing versions or saying Terms acceptance removes withdrawal rights.
- Acceptance confirmation: purpose, confirm the same document versions were accepted; attachments, acceptance record if sent separately; required evidence, affirmative acceptance and version match; CRM change, `offer_sent` to `client`; wording to avoid, backdating, implied consent, or missing version references.
- Early-start request: purpose, collect the separate request for work during the withdrawal period; attachments, early-start request; required evidence, explicit request and durable-medium copy; CRM change, keep `client`; wording to avoid, bundling it into Terms acceptance or implying it is mandatory.
- Payment confirmation: purpose, confirm receipt and the next step; attachments, receipt if helpful; required evidence, payment record; CRM change, keep `client`; wording to avoid, treating payment as scope expansion.
- Scope-change approval: purpose, confirm extra work or third-party costs before action; attachments, revised scope or cost list; required evidence, written approval; CRM change, keep `client`; wording to avoid, verbal-only approval or retroactive approval language.
- Completion: purpose, deliver the completed work and close the matter; attachments, final deliverable or summary; required evidence, delivery timestamp; CRM change, keep `client` or move to `closed` after wrap-up; wording to avoid, guaranteed external results.
- Complaint acknowledgement: purpose, confirm receipt and explain the review process; attachments, none unless needed for the complaint; required evidence, complaint log and reply; CRM change, usually `contacted` until resolved, then `closed`; wording to avoid, blame, defensiveness, or waiver language.
