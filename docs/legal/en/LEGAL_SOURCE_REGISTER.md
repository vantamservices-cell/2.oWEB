# Legal Source Register

Access date: 2026-06-16

This register contains only official sources used for the Cycle 2B English legal document foundation. It distinguishes binding law from official guidance. It is not legal advice.

| Authority | Title | Official URL | Proposition supported | Classification |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| European Union / EUR-Lex | Directive 2011/83/EU on consumer rights | https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:32011L0083 | Consumer distance service contracts require pre-contract information, withdrawal information, durable-medium confirmation, 14-day withdrawal framework, separate early performance request, and proportional payment rules after early start. | binding law |
| European Union / EUR-Lex | Directive (EU) 2023/2673 amending Directive 2011/83/EU as regards financial services contracts concluded at a distance and repealing Directive 2002/65/EC | https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/HTML/?uri=CELEX:32023L2673 | Article 1 amends Directive 2011/83/EU and inserts Article 11a. Article 11a requires, for distance contracts concluded by means of an online interface where a withdrawal right exists, an online withdrawal function that is clearly labelled, continuously available during the withdrawal period, prominent and easily accessible, captures the consumer name, contract-identifying details, and electronic acknowledgement destination, includes a separate confirmation function, and sends acknowledgement on a durable medium without undue delay. Article 2 requires Member States to apply the measures from 19 June 2026. | binding EU law requiring national transposition |
| European Union / EUR-Lex | Council Directive 93/13/EEC on unfair terms in consumer contracts | https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/?uri=CELEX:31993L0013 | Consumer terms must not create unfair imbalance; mandatory consumer protections cannot be removed by contract wording. | binding law |
| Dutch Government / Wetten.overheid.nl | Burgerlijk Wetboek Boek 6, including consumer distance-contract and general-conditions rules | https://wetten.overheid.nl/BWBR0005289/ | Dutch implementation context for contract terms, distance service contracts, general conditions, performance, and mandatory consumer protections. | binding law |
