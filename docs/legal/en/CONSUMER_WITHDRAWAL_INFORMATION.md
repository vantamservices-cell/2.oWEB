This information is intended for consumers who conclude a distance service contract with VANTAM Services. It should be supplied before the consumer is bound by the contract.

## 1. When the Withdrawal Right Generally Applies

If you are a consumer and you conclude a service contract with VANTAM at a distance, for example by email or another remote written acceptance process, you may have a statutory right to withdraw from the contract.

A consumer is a natural person acting for purposes outside that person's trade, business, craft, or profession.

Some statutory exceptions may apply. VANTAM must not assume an exception unless it fits the specific service and all legal conditions are satisfied.

## 2. Withdrawal Period and Starting Point

The usual withdrawal period for a consumer distance service contract is 14 days. The period normally starts on the day the service contract is concluded.

If legally required withdrawal information is not supplied correctly, the withdrawal period may be extended under applicable law.

## 3. How to Exercise the Right

To withdraw, you must inform VANTAM of your decision to withdraw from the contract by an unambiguous statement.

You may use the model withdrawal form, but you do not have to use it.

## 4. Online Withdrawal Function

Once implemented and legally applicable, the VANTAM online withdrawal function will provide an additional method of exercising the withdrawal right for a consumer who concluded the distance contract through VANTAM's online interface.

The online withdrawal function is additional to other lawful withdrawal methods. The model withdrawal form and an unambiguous written statement remain available.

Where legally required, the function must remain accessible throughout the withdrawal period. After the consumer submits a withdrawal through the function, VANTAM must send acknowledgement without undue delay on a durable medium.

This draft does not claim that the online withdrawal function is currently live.

## 5. Effect of Withdrawal

If you validly withdraw, VANTAM must repay payments received from you for the withdrawn contract where required by law.

Repayment must be made according to applicable law and the accepted agreement. If VANTAM has not yet started work and no lawful agreed consultation fee or other lawful upfront amount is payable, no service fee should be charged.

## 6. Work Performed After a Valid Early-Start Request

If you expressly requested VANTAM to begin performance during the withdrawal period and acknowledged that proportional payment may be due, VANTAM may charge a lawful proportion of the agreed total price for properly performed work before you withdrew.

The amount must be proportionate and must not exceed what applicable law allows. VANTAM must not invent a formula that charges more than a lawful proportion of the agreed total price.

Pre-approved third-party costs may be payable only where legally and contractually allowed.

## 7. Possible Loss of Withdrawal Right After Full Performance

For a service fully performed during the withdrawal period, possible loss of the withdrawal right applies only where all statutory conditions are satisfied. These may include your prior express request to start before the withdrawal period expires, your acknowledgement that the withdrawal right may be lost after full performance, and durable-medium confirmation.

General Terms acceptance alone is not enough for early start or loss of withdrawal right.

## 8. If Required Information Is Omitted

If VANTAM fails to provide legally required information about the withdrawal right, the withdrawal period may be extended under applicable law. VANTAM must not hide or contract around that consequence.

## 9. Relationship to Cancellation and Termination

Statutory withdrawal is separate from:

- cancellation before work begins;
- client termination after work begins;
- consultation rescheduling;
- VANTAM termination for non-payment, lack of cooperation, false information, illegality, safety, abuse, or conflict.

Mandatory consumer rights override any commercial wording that conflicts with them.
