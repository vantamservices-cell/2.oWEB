# VANTAM Product Architecture V3

## Positioning
VANTAM is practical support for international students from arrival planning to everyday student life in the Netherlands. The public site must help visitors understand who VANTAM is for, which product fits their situation, what tangible outputs they receive, and where scope stops.

## Binding Public Ladder
### Consultation
- Relocation Orientation — `€59`
- Duration: `45 minutes`
- Includes: situation assessment, personalised action sequence, written recap, product recommendation, `€59` credit toward `VANTAM Start` or `VANTAM First Year` within `14 days`
- This is the only public consultation card

### Main Packages
- VANTAM Start — `€449`
- VANTAM First Year — `€899` — `Recommended`
- Continue is not part of the public comparison; it remains a returning-client route only

### Housing
- Housing Ready — `€249`
- Housing Search Campaign — `€649`
- Housing stays separate from the main package comparison

### Individual Student Services
- Publicly grouped into four blocks:
- `A` Official & Administrative Problems
- `B` University, Work & Insurance
- `C` Housing Problems
- `D` Urgent & Major Life Changes

Use exactly four public situations:
1. `I am moving or have just arrived` → Start versus First Year
2. `I need housing now or later` → Housing Ready versus Housing Search Campaign
3. `I already live here and have a practical problem` → Individual Student Services
4. `I represent an organisation` → partner/contact route

1. Assess
2. Map

4. Verify & continue

## Product Rules
### VANTAM Start
- Outcome: essential first-month setup organised and brought to a clear completion point
- Before arrival: full intake, personalised Student Life Map, pre-arrival roadmap, essential-document readiness review, ordered before/after-arrival sequence
- After arrival: municipality registration and BSN process, DigiD, one bank-account process, appropriate insurance route, GP registration, one university or general-administration case, one Official Letter Action, one progress check, business-day support within the active period
- End deliverables: updated Student Life Map, Completed / Pending / Waiting overview, documents-to-retain list, next 90-day plan, Completion Pack
- Period: up to `35 days after arrival`, including defined pre-arrival preparation

### VANTAM First Year
- Outcome: get established during the first month and stay supported through key practical moments of the first academic year
- Includes everything in Start
- Adds:
- Three scheduled checkpoints: `90-Day Check`, `Mid-Year Check`, `Year-End Check`
- Three Action Credits
- Two Quick Reviews
- Housing Readiness Activation
- One Urgent Triage
- Optional Parent Update only with separate written consent from the adult student
- Final deliverables: updated Student Life Map, documented case history maintained during engagement, checkpoint recaps, year-end plan, next activation route
- No unlimited-support implication

### Housing Ready
- Outcome: a complete application-ready housing profile and the first qualified submissions
- Includes: budget and criteria review, realistic housing strategy, renter profile, guarantor positioning, document checklist, guarantor checklist, reusable introduction message, application pack, up to `three Qualified Applications`, basic fit check before each submission, one response/offer screen, final next-step plan
- Period: `30 days`
- Unused application slots may remain active for another `30 days` when no suitable properties were available

### Housing Search Campaign
- Outcome: a structured `six-week` housing campaign with a visible application pipeline and fixed execution limits
- Includes: housing strategy, affordability and criteria review, complete application pack, guarantor positioning, search-channel plan, up to `ten Qualified Applications`, Housing Pipeline, up to `three Active Lead Cases`, up to `two communication rounds per lead`, preparation for up to `two viewings`, viewing checklist, one Housing Offer & Contract Check, weekly pipeline update, campaign recap, check-in readiness checklist after success
- Optional additions use `Request a price` unless a specific owner-approved price exists

### Individual Student Services
- Fixed-price services only where scope is predictable
- Use `Request a price` where urgency, documents, parties, communication rounds, or history vary
- `Urgent Situation Assessment — €99` replaces the old public priority consultation and lives inside block `D`

## Typical Situations
Heading must be:
`Typical situations VANTAM can take into work`

Include exactly eight scenarios:
1. arrival in six weeks with no sequence
2. setup plus first-year support
3. rental profile plus first applications
4. active six-week campaign
5. CAK/SVB or municipality letter
6. academic warning or internship administration
7. urgent housing exit
8. graduation and orientation-year consideration

## Deliverables And Definitions
Visible deliverables:
- Student Life Map
- Case Card
- Application Pack
- Housing Pipeline
- Checkpoint Recap
- Completion Recap
- Next-Step Plan
- Deadline List
- Completion Pack

Definitions:
- Action Credit: one defined problem, one agreed outcome, one document set, up to two communication rounds, and a Completion Recap
- Quick Review: one small document/message review without drafting or third-party communication
- Qualified Application: suitable property, basic profile compatibility, ready file, and actual submission
- Active Lead Case: meaningful response, document request, viewing invitation, or discussion of terms
- Request a price: written scope, deliverables, exclusions, and price before work begins
