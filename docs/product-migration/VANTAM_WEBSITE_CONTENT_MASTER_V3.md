# VANTAM Website Content Master V3

## English Semantic Master
Practical support for international students from arrival planning to everyday student life in the Netherlands.

The homepage should make four things clear fast:
- who VANTAM is for
- which product fits the visitor’s situation
- what VANTAM will actually do
- what tangible output the visitor receives

## Hero Direction
- Address both pre-arrival and already-resident students
- Keep contact as the main navigation CTA
- Use calm, practical language
- Student Life Map is a working-document preview, not a portal

Suggested core message:
- Headline theme: practical support for international students from arrival to everyday student life in the Netherlands
- Subheadline theme: help with relocation steps, housing preparation, and defined first-year situations, without guaranteed outcomes or institutional language

## Section Content Rules
### Situation Selector
Use exactly these four situations and route them to the correct section plus contact prefill:
- `I am moving or have just arrived`
- `I need housing now or later`
- `I already live here and have a practical problem`
- `I represent an organisation`

### How VANTAM Works
Use the four stages only:
- Assess
- Map
- Act & coordinate
- Verify & continue

### Start Versus First Year
- Show only `VANTAM Start` and `VANTAM First Year`
- Mark `VANTAM First Year` as `Recommended`
- Keep cards compact and details structured
- Make the difference clear: Start is first-month execution; First Year is Start plus defined year support
- Continue must not appear as an equal public package

### Relocation Orientation
- One public consultation only
- `€59`
- `45 minutes`
- situation assessment
- personalised action sequence
- written recap
- product recommendation
- `€59` credit within `14 days`

### Housing Ready Versus Housing Search Campaign
- Keep housing separate from main packages
- Use exact fixed periods and limits
- Use `Request a price` for optional additions where approved pricing is not public

### Individual Student Services
- Present four blocks `A–D`
- Use exact approved services, fixed prices, and `Request a price` states
- Tighten wording to practical review, sequence, preparation, communication, and handoff
- Remove weak standalone services from public display

### Student Life Map Preview
- Separate section, not merged into process
- Show seven areas and six statuses
- Copy must clearly say illustrative only

### Typical Situations
Heading must be exactly:
`Typical situations VANTAM can take into work`

Each scenario should show:
- Trigger
- What is at risk
- What VANTAM does
- Recommended product
- Boundary

No fictional names, universities, cities, quotes, or testimonial framing.

## Product Copy Rules
### VANTAM Start
Use the outcome, before-arrival scope, after-arrival scope, deliverables, and `up to 35 days after arrival` period from Master V3.

### VANTAM First Year
Use the outcome, three checkpoints, three Action Credits, two Quick Reviews, Housing Readiness Activation, one Urgent Triage, optional Parent Update by written consent, and final deliverables from Master V3.

### Continue
- Remove from the main package array and comparison
- Keep only as returning-client note, FAQ mention, and contact route
- No detailed public scope or price

### Housing Ready
- Keep `€249`
- Mention `up to three Qualified Applications`
- Mention `30 days`

### Housing Search Campaign
- Keep `€649`
- Mention `six weeks`
- Mention `up to ten Qualified Applications`
- Mention `up to three Active Lead Cases`
- Mention `up to two communication rounds per lead`
- Mention preparation for `up to two viewings`

## Pricing Rules
- Fixed price only where Master V3 allows it
- Use `Request a price` where scope varies
- Do not use:
- `After assessment`
- `from`
- public hourly rates
- hidden mandatory additions

## Deliverables And Definitions
Show tangible outputs consistently:
- Student Life Map
- Case Card
- Application Pack
- Housing Pipeline
- Checkpoint Recap
- Completion Recap
- Next-Step Plan
- Deadline List
- Completion Pack

Use the same definitions everywhere for:
- Action Credit
- Quick Review
- Qualified Application
- Active Lead Case
- Request a price

## Multilingual Rules
English is the semantic master.

Ukrainian and Russian must be natural while staying identical to English on:
- prices
- quantities
- periods
- exclusions
- optional/add-on logic
- CTA destination
- section hierarchy
- service grouping

Translate all public UI:
- headings
- navigation
- buttons
- tabs
- badges
- modal text
- form options
- validation feedback
- print/save wording
- accessibility labels

## Do Not Use
- Parent Briefing as an equal public consultation
- Priority Situation Call as a consultation
- Continue as a main package card
- old service map/planner framing
- generic planner presented as Student Life Map
- fictional testimonial language
- generated-PDF claims
- portal/dashboard language
- mixed-language fragments
